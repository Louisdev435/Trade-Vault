📘 How TradeVault Works

1 Getting Started

  • Set your portfolio balance - Click "Update Balance" in sidebar to set your starting account balance
  • Add your first trade - Click the button anywhere
  • All data saves automatically in your browser - no server needed

2 Adding Trades (Trade Log)

  • Date - Select trade date
  • Symbol - Enter instrument name (e.g., ETHUSD, AAPL)
  • Direction - Choose Long (buy) or Short (sell)
  • Position Size - Enter number of units/contracts
  • Entry/Exit - Your opening and closing prices
  • Stop Loss / Take Profit - Optional levels
  • Win/Loss Toggle - Click Win (✅) or Loss (❌) to set trade result
  • P/L auto-calculates based on size and price difference
  • Screenshot - Paste any image (Ctrl+V) or click to upload
  • Notes - Add your trade thoughts

3 Dashboard Features

  • Total Net P/L - Your overall profit/loss from all trades
  • Profit Factor = Gross Profit / Gross Loss. Above 1.5 is good, above 2.0 is excellent
  • Today's P/L - Performance for current day
  • Today's Trades - Number of trades today
  • Equity Curve - Graph shows portfolio growth (green up, red down)
  • Win/Loss Analysis - Circle chart showing your win rate
  • Recent Trades - Last 7 trades preview

4 Trade History

  • View-only table of all your trades
  • Half-circle meter shows win rate visually
  • Equity curve with date range filter
  • Growth Calculator - Click the black "RESULTS" button to calculate growth between any two points

5 Calendar

  • Green days - Profitable trading days
  • Red days - Losing trading days
  • Click day to see trades on that date
  • Date range filter to analyze specific periods

6 Risk Calculator

  • Risk Calculator - Enter your account balance and select risk % (0.5%, 1%, 2%)
  • Position Size Calculator - Enter entry, stop loss, take profit, and risk amount
  • Fee Analysis - Shows market vs limit order fee impact

7 Monte Carlo Simulation NEW

  • What it does: Takes your trade history and runs thousands of simulations to predict possible futures
  • Upload your trades - CSV file with P/L column
  • Run 10,000+ simulations to see probability of profit and risk of ruin
  • 95% VaR (Value at Risk) - Worst 5% of outcomes you might face
  • Probability of Ruin - Chance your account hits zero. If it's >5%, reduce position size!
  • Fan Chart - Shows median path and 90% confidence interval

🎲 Simple Explanation:

Imagine putting all your past trades in a bag and drawing them randomly 100 times. The Monte Carlo simulation does this 10,000 times to show you: What's the BEST case? What's the WORST case? How likely are you to go broke? This helps you adjust your risk before it's too late.

8 Backup System IMPORTANT

  • Your trades are ALREADY saved automatically in your browser (localStorage)
  • Save Backup File - Creates a .json file with ALL your data (trades, portfolio, settings)
  • Restore From Backup - Upload a previous backup to recover lost data
  • When to backup: Before clearing browser history, after adding important trades, weekly for safety
  • Keep ONE master backup and update it regularly - don't create multiple versions

💾 Why Backup?

Your trading data lives in your browser. If you clear cookies/cache, everything disappears. A backup file is your insurance policy. Save it to your computer, Google Drive, or USB drive. If you ever lose data, one click restores everything!

Pro Tips:
  • Use Ctrl+V to paste screenshots directly into trade log
  • Export backup before clearing browser history
  • Check Profit Factor weekly to track your edge
  • Use date filters to analyze specific trading periods
  • Run Monte Carlo after every 20 trades to see if your risk is still safe
  • If Probability of Ruin > 5%, reduce your position size by half
