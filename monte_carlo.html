The Trading Truth Machine

Why use a Monte Carlo Simulation?

🎲 The Simple Explanation:

Think of this as a 'Stress Test' for your trading strategy. We take your past trades, put them in a bag, and shake them up. We then simulate 10,000 different versions of your future to see if your account would survive the 'unluckiest' streaks.

Example: If you have 10 trades with 6 wins and 4 losses, we randomly pick from these 10 trades (like drawing from a deck of cards) to create 10,000 different possible futures. Some futures might have 8 wins in a row, others might have 5 losses in a row. This shows you what could really happen.

How this helps you make money long-term:

🛡️

1. Survival Check

It calculates your 'Probability of Ruin.' If the math shows even a 1% chance of hitting zero, you are risking too much. Fix this, and you stay in the game forever.

Example: If you risk 10% per trade, you might have a 15% chance of going bankrupt. The simulation will show you this danger before it happens.

😌

2. Fear Management

It shows you the 'Maximum Drawdown' you might face. When you eventually hit a losing streak in real life, you won't panic because you've already seen it in the simulation.

Example: The simulation might show you could lose 30% of your account at some point. When it happens in real life, you'll know it's normal and stick to your plan.

⚖️

3. Honest Judgment

This is a machine that is 100% honest. It removes your ego and tells you if your strategy is a professional business or just a lucky gamble.

Example: You might think you're a great trader after 5 wins in a row, but the simulation might show that 5-win streaks happen by chance 40% of the time with your strategy.

📌

Why is this important? Most traders fail because they run out of money during a bad streak. This tool tells you exactly how much money you need to survive those streaks so you can reach your long-term goals.

The Bottom Line: A profitable strategy isn't enough. You need to know if you can survive the inevitable losing streaks. Monte Carlo simulation answers that question with math, not emotions.

Monte Carlo Guide

📊 How Monte Carlo Works

The Monte Carlo simulation randomly resamples your trade history thousands of times to show possible future outcomes.

1

Upload Your Trades

Upload a CSV or Excel file with your trade history. The file should have a column with your P/L (profit/loss) per trade.

2

Map Your Columns

Tell us which column contains your P/L data. Optional: date and direction columns help with grouping.

3

Run Simulation

We'll run thousands of simulations to show you the range of possible outcomes, probability of profit, and risk metrics.

💡 Understanding the Results

  • Expected Return: The median outcome - half of simulations end above this, half below
  • Probability of Profit: What percentage of simulations end with a profit
  • 95% VaR (Value at Risk): The worst 5% of outcomes - you have a 95% chance of doing better than this
  • Confidence Intervals: Show the range at different probability levels
  • Fan Chart: Shows the median path (center line) with 90% confidence interval (shaded area)
